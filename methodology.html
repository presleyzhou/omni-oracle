  • Ranjan & Gneiting (2010) JRSS-B — combining probability forecasts
  • Gneiting & Raftery (2007) JASA — proper scoring rules; Gneiting et al. (2007) JRSS-B — calibration & sharpness

Prediction markets → P1–P8 & C5

  • Wolfers & Zitzewitz (2004) JEP; NBER w12083 — accuracy surveys; price ≈ belief-weighted probability

  • Hanson (2003, 2007) — logarithmic market scoring rules (LMSR)
  • Othman et al. — liquidity-sensitive LMSR; Chen et al. — combinatorial market makers
  • arXiv 2503.03312 — manipulability; 2604.24366 — Polymarket microstructure; 2605.30802 — multi-agent AI oracles; 2606.04217 — Polymarket-v1 database

Market infrastructure (C5) — design choices

Matching: hybrid CLOB — off-chain order matching, auditable on-chain settlement; binary complementary tokens with the invariant 1 YES + 1 NO = $1; mint/merge on crossed complementary orders (Polymarket/Gnosis CTF pattern). Liquidity: Hanson LMSR seeds thin markets with bounded loss b·log n; CLOB takes over as volume grows. Resolution: three tiers — automated data feeds, optimistic oracle (bond → dispute window → vote) with escalating bonds and criteria locked at market creation, and an expert council for appeals; AI adjudication only as dispute triage. Manipulation resistance: position limits near resolution, order-flow anomaly detection, public post-mortems.

Data sources

Macro (M1): FRED / FRED-MD, ALFRED vintages, BEA, BLS, Census, ECB SDW, OECD, SPF, Blue Chip, CME FedWatch, TIPS breakevens, news/text corpora, alt-data (card spend, job postings, mobility).

Equities & innovation (M2): USPTO PatentsView, Google Patents, SEC EDGAR full-text, earnings-call transcripts, CRSP/Compustat-class market data, arXiv, PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, Crunchbase, GitHub activity.

Markets (P1–P8): official results feeds (elections, leagues, awards), medianized exchange price feeds (crypto), BLS/BEA/Fed releases, documented-event criteria with locked resolution wording.

Roadmap

  • Phase 1 (0–6 mo): M1 nowcasting core (DFM + random forest on FRED-MD), scoring layer (C3), play-money tournament on econ-release questions.
  • Phase 2 (6–12 mo): M2 pipeline (patents + GKX-style ranking), P2/P3/P7 markets with LMSR seeding, ensemble engine v1.
  • Phase 3 (12–24 mo): full CLOB + oracle stack, remaining categories, conditional/combinatorial markets, institutional API GA.
